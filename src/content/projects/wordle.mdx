The function calculating expected information gain runs in O(GA) time,
where G is the number of guesses (12,974), and A is the number of
answers (2,309).

This is calculated once per guess, and since all possible answers are
simulated, the total runtime to find the average guesses required is
O(A²G).

Caching is hence critical, and is implemented with a tree structure
with guesses and outcomes (🟩🟩🟩🟨🟨) as nodes.
